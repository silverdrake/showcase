THE GRIN



The Grin is a Doom2 episode I created in 2010 to work on my level design skills.
I promise I'll add the full story of this level set and its design rationale and such, for now I'll just say the whole thing was created in a month's time and everything I made after version 0.9.0 was bug fixing to adapt to new versions of the various Doom ports I tested it on (for example, on most recent versions of GZDoom I found a secret passage broke because the space to pass through it became too narrow, I guess it was a rounding issue of some kind, which is why you have a 0.9.5 version made 7 years after the original).
Part of the "challenge" was that I wanted to see how I progressed so no level would be re-designed after I moved to the next one. So you can expect the first ones to be a bit limited; level 1 in particular but it lasts a minute or so, the others until level 4 are simpler in graphics and atmosphere than what comes next but should be fun enough, and the actual "meat" of wad starts on level 5, this second part being what I enjoyed it.
It's still a sort of "first wad" for me, I had made a few levels in the 90s but they were devoid of any "proper" design, and I hung up my editor for a long time after that. Hope you enjoy these anyway.

System Requirements

Any modern Doom port should do, but you need to have the original game data (you can find the game on GOG or Steam at a very low price nowadays):
Doom2 (doom2.wad)
needed to run the levels at all.
Doom (doom.wad)
needed to get the proper background music for some levels (mixed up the original order), use it as a PWAD in the launcher.
I'm assuming you know how to launch Doom with some extra files or have a loader in place, but here's an example from my Linux box ($PATH_IWADS being a directory where you have both the original Doom WADS), with GZDoom launched from the directory where you unpack the zips:
GZDoom
gzdoom -wnd -iwad $PATH_IWADS/doom2.wad -file $PATH_IWADS/doom.wad graphtx3.wad nb_flats.wad NB_RECOL.WAD q1tex.wad TheGrin-0.9.5.1.wad
